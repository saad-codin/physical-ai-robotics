# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

Welcome to the AI-Native Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook! This comprehensive guide will take you through the fundamental concepts and advanced techniques needed to understand and develop humanoid robots.

## What You'll Learn

This textbook is organized into four core modules with advanced topics:

1. **ROS 2 Fundamentals** - Master the Robot Operating System 2, the backbone of modern robotics development
   - Core concepts: nodes, topics, services, and actions
   - Advanced topics: lifecycle management, parameters, and launch systems
   - Practical applications: building distributed robotic systems

2. **Digital Twin & Simulation** - Create accurate virtual models of physical robots for testing and development
   - Simulation platforms: Gazebo, Webots, Isaac Sim, and Unity Robotics
   - Modeling techniques: URDF, physics properties, and sensor simulation
   - Digital twin synchronization: real-time data exchange and validation

3. **AI-Robot Brain** - Develop perception, planning, and control systems for autonomous robots
   - Perception systems: computer vision, sensor fusion, and environmental understanding
   - Planning algorithms: motion planning, task planning, and hierarchical approaches
   - Control systems: feedback control, adaptive control, and learning-based methods

4. **Vision Language Action (VLA)** - Build systems that can perceive, understand, and act on complex tasks
   - VLA architectures: unified transformers, modular systems, and hierarchical control
   - Implementation strategies: real-time processing, cloud-edge architectures, and safety considerations
   - Advanced applications: natural language interaction and multimodal reasoning

## Advanced Topics

The textbook also covers specialized advanced topics:

- **Humanoid Control**: Balance control, walking pattern generation, and whole-body control
- **Manipulation**: Grasp planning, force control, and dexterous manipulation
- **Navigation**: Path planning, SLAM, and multi-robot coordination
- **Multi-Robot Systems**: Coordination protocols, task allocation, and distributed control
